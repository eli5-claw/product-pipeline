# Monetization Patterns

## SaaS Pricing Tiers

### Standard Three-Tier
- **Starter**: $29/mo — Individual, basic features
- **Professional**: $79/mo — Team, advanced features, priority support
- **Enterprise**: $199+/mo — Custom, dedicated support, SLA

### Usage-Based Examples
- **OpenAI**: Pay per token
- **Twilio**: Pay per SMS/call
- **AWS**: Pay per compute/storage
- **Stripe**: 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction

### Freemium Conversion Models
- **Slack**: Free → Paid at 10K messages
- **Notion**: Free → Paid for guests/advanced features
- **Figma**: Free → Paid for teams/projects
- **GitHub**: Free → Paid for private repos/advanced features

## Marketplace Take Rates

| Platform | Take Rate | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Uber | 20-25% | Varies by market |
| Airbnb | 14-16% | Split between host/guest |
| App Store | 15-30% | 15% under $1M, 30% after |
| Stripe Connect | 0.25% + platform fee | Infrastructure layer |
| Shopify | 2.4% + $29/mo | Plus transaction fees |

## Revenue Benchmarks

### Healthy SaaS Metrics
- **LTV/CAC ratio**: > 3x
- **Payback period**: < 12 months
- **Net Revenue Retention**: > 100% (expansion > churn)
- **Gross Margin**: > 70%
- **Monthly Churn**: < 5% (B2B), < 10% (B2C)

### Pricing Psychology Tactics
- **Charm pricing**: $29 not $30
- **Anchoring**: Show enterprise tier first
- **Decoy effect**: Add a tier nobody buys
- **Bundling**: Combine features for perceived value
- **Annual discount**: 2 months free for annual pay
